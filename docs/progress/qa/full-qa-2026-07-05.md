exactly twice** as final adjudicator on the hardest judgments.

file fully read), hunting honesty/vacuity, statement fidelity, docstring drift, dead code,
duplication. Plus 8 cross-cutting auditors: rename residue, axiom-audit coverage, import
graph (scripted reachability from `ErgodicTheory.lean`), docs truthfulness, blueprint
`\lean{}` integrity, CI/infra, hygiene inventory, cross-module duplication. Every
critical/major finding got an individual adversarial verifier (prompted to refute); minors
batch-verified. Result: 79 findings (1 major, 59 confirmed minors, 1 refuted, 1 uncertain,
17 unverified nits). A completeness critic then identified the blind spots that drove
round 2.

**Round 2 — depth pass (24 agent runs).** Six definition-fidelity auditors unfolded the
flagship conclusion predicates to plain mathematics; six hunters ran the critic's follow-ups
(vacuity re-sweep ×2, conditional-hypothesis discharge ledger, mechanical docstring-reference
sweep, Lieb-spine and Petz-spine deep-math re-verification against Carlen / Petz 2003), with
the same adversarial verification. **Fable adjudicated the MET side and the quantum side in
two calls**, re-deriving every contested reading from source (including its own from-scratch
audit of the KS/Ruelle pair after the Opus report for it was lost to a structured-output
failure — no coverage gap).

Totals: 71 subagent invocations, ~6.3 M subagent tokens, ~80 minutes wall-clock.

## 5. Major findings

### 5.1 M1 — vacuous refining-limit pair (`Multifractal`)

`ErgodicTheory/Multifractal/RefiningLimit.lean:80` (`renyiDim_uniform_tendsto_dim`) and
`ErgodicTheory/Multifractal/Source/FlowPartition.lean:263`
(`refiningLimitConvergesProp_of_uniform`) both hypothesize

```
hcard : ∀ ε ∈ Set.Ioo (0:ℝ) 1, (Fintype.card (ι ε) : ℝ) = ε ^ (-d)
```

for a single family `ι : ℝ → Type`. An ℕ-valued cardinality cannot equal the continuous,
strictly monotone `ε ^ (-d)` at *every* ε of a continuum unless `d = 0` — so both `Tendsto`
conclusions are vacuously true for every nontrivial dimension. Twice independently
adversarially verified (constructive impossibility argument). The genuine content of the
module lives in the *per-resolution* lemmas (`renyiDim_uniform_eq_dim`, satisfiable at any
single ε with ε^(−d) ∈ ℕ), which the docstrings should point to. Aggravators: both vacuous
decls are guarded in `test/AxiomAudit.lean` (the axiom guard gives zero vacuity assurance),
and the docstrings advertise a "dyadic-grid model of d-dimensional Lebesgue measure" that can
never be instantiated at positive d. Mitigators: blast radius is confirmed **contained** —
no consumer, no README/STATE headline feeds on them. *Fix:* restate on a sequence
(εₙ = 2⁻ⁿ, card = 2ⁿᵈ — satisfiable) or delete the tendsto pair and keep the per-resolution
lemmas; update the two docstrings and the AxiomAudit entries.

### 5.2 M2 — "CPTP data-processing inequality" overstatement (living docs)

What the library proves (and states with scrupulous in-source honesty):

1. the **fully general partial-trace DPI** — `RelEntropyMonotoneUnderPartialTrace`
   discharged by `relEntropyMonotone_partialTrace` (`DataProcessingGeneral.lean:177`) for
   all finite product types, arbitrary ρ, faithful σ; and
2. the **faithful-ancilla Stinespring family** extension
   `monotonicity_relEntropy_under_stinespring` (`DataProcessingCPTP.lean:64`):
   Λρ = Tr_e(U(ρ⊗α)Uᴴ) with α PosDef — a proper subclass of CPTP that excludes
   pure-ancilla channels such as amplitude damping.

No DPI for an arbitrary `KrausChannel` exists in-repo, and the module docstring says so
explicitly. But `README.md:34` headlines "the **CPTP data-processing inequality**
(`monotonicity_relEntropy_under_CPTP`)" — an unqualified general-CPTP claim *citing the
deprecated alias whose 2026-07-04 rename existed precisely to remove that overstatement* —
and `CLAUDE.md:52,63` and `docs/progress/STATE.md:78` repeat the unqualified label. A reader
would believe a general CPTP DPI is formalized; it is not. *Fix:* one-line rewording in the
three living docs ("partial-trace + faithful-ancilla-Stinespring DPI") and cite the canonical
name. (Note: the Petz-equality theorems are unaffected — they take monotonicity as an
explicit hypothesis or use the channels for which it is proved.)

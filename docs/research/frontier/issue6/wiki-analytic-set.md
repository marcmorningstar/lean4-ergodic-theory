For the (unrelated) notion of analytic sets in geometry, see [analytic space](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Analytic_space "Analytic space")
.

In the mathematical field of [descriptive set theory](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Descriptive_set_theory "Descriptive set theory")
, a subset of a [Polish space](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polish_space "Polish space")
 X {\\displaystyle X} ![{\displaystyle X}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/68baa052181f707c662844a465bfeeb135e82bab) is an **analytic set** if it is a [continuous](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Continuous_(mathematics) "Continuous (mathematics)")
 image of a Polish space. These sets were first defined by [Luzin (1917)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Analytic_set#CITEREFLuzin1917)
 and his student [Souslin (1917)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Analytic_set#CITEREFSouslin1917)
.[\[1\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Analytic_set#cite_note-1)

Definition
----------

[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Analytic_set&action=edit&section=1 "Edit section: Definition")

There are several equivalent definitions of analytic set. The following conditions on a [subspace](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Space_(mathematics) "Space (mathematics)")
 _A_ of a Polish space _X_ are equivalent:

*   _A_ is analytic.
*   _A_ is [empty](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Empty_set "Empty set")
     or a continuous image of the [Baire space](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Baire_space_(set_theory) "Baire space (set theory)")
     ωω.
*   _A_ is a [Suslin space](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Suslin_space "Suslin space")
    , in other words _A_ is the image of a Polish space under a continuous mapping.
*   _A_ is the continuous image of a [Borel set](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Borel_set "Borel set")
     in a Polish space.
*   _A_ is a [Suslin set](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Suslin_set "Suslin set")
    , the image of the [Suslin operation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Suslin_operation "Suslin operation")
    .
*   There is a Polish space Y {\\displaystyle Y} ![{\displaystyle Y}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/961d67d6b454b4df2301ac571808a3538b3a6d3f) and a [Borel](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Borel_algebra "Borel algebra")
     set B ⊆ X × Y {\\displaystyle B\\subseteq X\\times Y} ![{\displaystyle B\subseteq X\times Y}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/7bca5667518759a85b20840b66b1a993441f944c) such that A {\\displaystyle A} ![{\displaystyle A}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/7daff47fa58cdfd29dc333def748ff5fa4c923e3) is the [projection](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Projection_(measure_theory) "Projection (measure theory)")
     of B {\\displaystyle B} ![{\displaystyle B}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/47136aad860d145f75f3eed3022df827cee94d7a) onto X {\\displaystyle X} ![{\displaystyle X}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/68baa052181f707c662844a465bfeeb135e82bab) ; that is,

A \= { x ∈ X | ( ∃ y ∈ Y ) ⟨ x , y ⟩ ∈ B } . {\\displaystyle A=\\{x\\in X|(\\exists y\\in Y)\\langle x,y\\rangle \\in B\\}.} ![{\displaystyle A=\{x\in X|(\exists y\in Y)\langle x,y\rangle \in B\}.}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/57c2527f98a7abad25c773563f6271e3d28f5568) 

*   _A_ is the projection of a [closed set](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Closed_set "Closed set")
     in the [cartesian product](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cartesian_product "Cartesian product")
     of _X_ with the Baire space.
*   _A_ is the projection of a [Gδ set](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/G%CE%B4_set "Gδ set")
     in the cartesian product of _X_ with the [Cantor space](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cantor_space "Cantor space")
     2ω.

An alternative characterization, in the specific, important, case that X {\\displaystyle X} ![{\displaystyle X}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/68baa052181f707c662844a465bfeeb135e82bab) is Baire space ωω, is that the analytic sets are precisely the projections of [trees](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tree_(descriptive_set_theory) "Tree (descriptive set theory)")
 on ω × ω {\\displaystyle \\omega \\times \\omega } ![{\displaystyle \omega \times \omega }](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/1982b5f82f795ffef6e49b5f08db982ce0c77749) . Similarly, the analytic subsets of Cantor space 2ω are precisely the projections of trees on 2 × ω {\\displaystyle 2\\times \\omega } ![{\displaystyle 2\times \omega }](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/10c3e0c19116cb5285d2df1f73be52dbbab9fd1b) .

Properties
----------

[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Analytic_set&action=edit&section=2 "Edit section: Properties")

Analytic subsets of Polish spaces are closed under countable unions and intersections, continuous images, and inverse images. The complement of an analytic set need not be analytic. Suslin proved that if the complement of an analytic set is analytic then the set is Borel. (Conversely any Borel set is analytic and Borel sets are closed under complements.) Luzin proved more generally that any two [disjoint](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Disjoint_sets "Disjoint sets")
 analytic sets are separated by a Borel set: in other words there is a Borel set [including](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Set_inclusion "Set inclusion")
 one and disjoint from the other. This is sometimes called the "Luzin separability principle" (though it was implicit in the proof of Suslin's theorem).

Analytic sets are always [Lebesgue measurable](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lebesgue_measurable "Lebesgue measurable")
 (indeed, [universally measurable](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Universally_measurable "Universally measurable")
) and have the [property of Baire](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Property_of_Baire "Property of Baire")
 and the [perfect set property](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Perfect_set_property "Perfect set property")
.

Examples
--------

[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Analytic_set&action=edit&section=3 "Edit section: Examples")

When A {\\displaystyle A} ![{\displaystyle A}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/7daff47fa58cdfd29dc333def748ff5fa4c923e3) is a set of natural numbers, refer to the set { x − y ∣ y ≤ x ∧ x , y ∈ A } {\\displaystyle \\{x-y\\mid y\\leq x\\land x,y\\in A\\}} ![{\displaystyle \{x-y\mid y\leq x\land x,y\in A\}}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/4d65f2c77a804843320e2a4fc55e33ad6b37e79f) as the difference set of A {\\displaystyle A} ![{\displaystyle A}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/7daff47fa58cdfd29dc333def748ff5fa4c923e3) . The set of difference sets of natural numbers is an analytic set, and is complete for analytic sets.[\[2\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Analytic_set#cite_note-2)
